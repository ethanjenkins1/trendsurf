# Microsoft Employee — Social Media Communication Guidelines

## Purpose
These guidelines ensure that social media posts authored by Microsoft employees are professional, on-brand, accurate, and appropriate for public sharing. Content is generated from trending topics and must reflect the values and standards of a Microsoft representative.

## Brand Voice
- **Empowering & Inclusive** — celebrate what developers and communities can achieve together
- **Technically credible** — demonstrate deep expertise without being inaccessible
- **Optimistic & Solution-oriented** — frame challenges as opportunities for innovation
- **Authentic & Human** — write like a real person, not a press release
- **Data-grounded** — support claims with sources and evidence

## Target Audience
- Software engineers, developers, and open-source contributors
- Engineering managers, CTOs, and technical decision-makers
- IT professionals, platform engineers, and SREs
- AI/ML practitioners and researchers
- Security professionals and CISOs
- Student developers and community advocates

## Approved Topic Areas
- Open-source software, InnerSource, and developer community
- AI, machine learning, responsible AI, and Copilot innovations
- Cloud-native development, Kubernetes, DevOps, and platform engineering
- Developer productivity and tooling (VS Code, .NET, TypeScript, GitHub)
- Cybersecurity, Zero Trust, and application security
- Sustainability in tech and green software engineering
- Industry trends, frameworks, and best practices
- Any trending technology topic relevant to the Microsoft + GitHub ecosystem

## Prohibited Language & Topics
- ❌ Disparaging competitors by name or implying superiority over specific products
- ❌ Unverified statistics or claims without source attribution
- ❌ Informal slang, memes, or overly casual tone that undermines credibility
- ❌ Political opinions or partisan commentary
- ❌ Confidential Microsoft information, internal roadmaps, or pre-announcement details
- ❌ Unsubstantiated claims about AI capabilities (e.g., "our AI is 100% accurate")
- ❌ Customer names or case studies without explicit approval
- ❌ Financial advice, stock commentary, or investment recommendations
- ❌ Speaking on behalf of all of Microsoft — clarify that opinions are your own

## Required Disclaimers
- **Opinion content**: "Views expressed are my own and do not necessarily reflect those of Microsoft."
- **AI/technology content**: "AI-assisted content — reviewed for accuracy before publishing."
- **Data/research content**: "Sources cited for verification. Always refer to original publications for full context."

## Platform Guidelines

### LinkedIn
- Max 1300 characters
- Thought-leadership tone, personal perspective welcome
- 3–5 relevant hashtags
- Include a clear CTA (e.g., "What's your experience?", "Check out the repo", "Read the full post")
- Tag relevant people, projects, or organizations when appropriate

### X/Twitter
- Max 280 characters
- Punchy, concise, insight-driven
- 1–2 hashtags maximum
- Link to long-form content or repos when possible

### Microsoft Teams (Internal)
- Digest format with bullet points
- Include action items and owners
- Reference internal resources, docs, and repos
- More detail than external posts — include technical depth

## Tone Examples

### Good Example
"The latest update to the NIST AI Risk Management Framework has real implications for how we build responsible AI systems. Here are 3 takeaways every engineering team should discuss. What's your team doing differently? #ResponsibleAI #AI"

### Bad Example
"OMG the new NIST rules are going to DESTROY companies that aren't ready!! Better get on board NOW or you'll be left behind! #FOMO"
